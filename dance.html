Dance

I danced. I danced alone for so long that my legs began to hurt. Fortunately, a break came upon and I could spare few minutes to rest. My brother, even though his age was little to understand the nature of our situation, sat beside me and started pressing my legs.

"Didi, we can leave now. See the red spots on your feet. Is it necessary to get more scars when your heart is tarnished with plenty?"

"But if I leave now, I will be paid half. To heal any scar, we need money, dear."

"You speak, but not fluidly. In between, the pain pounding inside causes your mouth to shiver."

"Aarav.." pain rolled through my eyes before I could say more. He absorbed the drops of it by slowly wiping them with his warm hands.

"I know Di, it's hard to live after what happened. Each morning, I wake up with night filled in my eyes too. We will figure out some way to live without ma and baa. What would they have said if they saw you like this?"

"They are dead, Aarav."

Stage lights shined again, and narrator of the act shouted my name. I blinked out of the moment, checked my makeup, and pressed my legs against the floor. I felt a surge of pain pass through my nerves each time my feets touched the floor but I continued to walk. I cannot stop. I cannot stop.

"Di, please."

"All I know is to dance when the beat drops."
